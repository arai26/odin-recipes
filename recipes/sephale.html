Description

Sephale is a traditional Nepali stuffed bread, often enjoyed as a snack or light meal. The dough is made from all-purpose flour, water, and a pinch of salt, kneaded into a soft, pliable texture. It is then rolled out into small discs, filled with a savory mixture of minced meat (usually chicken, pork, or buffalo), finely chopped onions, garlic, ginger, and spices like cumin and coriander. Once filled, the dough is folded and sealed, creating a half-moon shape. The stuffed bread is then deep-fried until golden and crispy on the outside, while the inside remains juicy and flavorful. Sephale is typically served hot, accompanied by a tangy tomato chutney or achar, enhancing its rich, savory taste.

    Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • Water (as needed for kneading)
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 inch ginger, minced
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin powder
  • Salt to taste
