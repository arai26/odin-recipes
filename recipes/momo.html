Description

Nepali momo is a popular dumpling dish that combines a soft, doughy exterior with a flavorful, savory filling. The dough is made from flour, water, and a pinch of salt, rolled into thin, circular wrappers. The filling typically includes minced meat (such as chicken, pork, or buffalo), finely chopped onions, garlic, ginger, and a mix of spices like cumin, coriander, and turmeric, all blended together for a rich, aromatic taste. Vegetarians often substitute the meat with finely chopped vegetables or paneer. Once filled, the momos are carefully pleated and steamed until the dough turns translucent.     Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • Water (as needed for kneading)
  • 250 grams minced meat (or finely chopped vegetables for a vegetarian version)
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 large tomatoes, blanched and peeled
  • 1-2 dried red chilies
